## Biot-Savart Law

Moving charges create a magnetic field along the wire.

The magnetic field at a point is defined as a vector tangent to the magnetic field lines (you get these from the curl right hand rule), and perpendicular to the wire.

## Lorentz Force

Given a magnetic field $\vec{B}$, created by a source or magnets, charges moving inside the field will experience a force:

$$\Large \vec{F_B}=q\vec{v}×\vec{B}$$

or if instead of vectors we use scalar quantities at an angle:

$$\Large F_b=|q|vB\sin\theta$$

> [!hint] Lorentz  force for current-carrying wires
> $$\Large F_b = I\vec{L}\times \vec{B}$$
> or
> $$\Large F_b = ILB \sin\theta$$
> 
> Where:
> - L is the length of the wire
> - $I$ is the current
> - B is the magnetic field
> - $\theta$ is the angle between the **direction of the current (the length vector $\vec{L}$) and the magnetic field ($\vec{B}$)**.
