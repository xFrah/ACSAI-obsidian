Recall that work is the "accumulated" force exerted through a distance.
What if we wanted a **work function** that tells us **how much work is needed to get back to equilibrium**?
$$\Large U(x)=-\int F(x)\;dx$$
Where $x$ is the distance from equlibrium / reference point.

Or the difference in potential energy is precisely the negative work done while going from potential energy 1 to potential energy 2.

> [!hint] Potential energy for gravity
> $$\Large U=\underbracket{mg}_{F}\underbracket{h}_{x}$$
> You can do this without integral because the force is constant.

> [!hint] Potential energy for a spring
> Assume we have a compressed string.
> 0 is the position we are at, $\Delta x$ is the distance that the spring will cover when released.
> 
> When released, the spring will exert $F_s$ through distance $\Delta x$:
> 
> $$\Large U=-\int^{\Delta x}_{0}F_s\;dx = -\int^{\Delta x}_{0}-kx\;dx=\frac{1}{2}k\underbracket{x}_{\Delta x}^2$$
