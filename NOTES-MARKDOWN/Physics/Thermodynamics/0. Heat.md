Energy (Joules) transferred between two systems due to a [[0. Internal energy and Temperature|temperature]] difference.
It is energy that flows from the region of higher temperature to the region of lower temperature and **not a property a system possesses on its own**.

Heat contributes to changes in a system's [[0. Internal energy and Temperature|internal energy]].

In a mechanical sense, it's the transfer of kinetic energy from one set of particles to another.

> [!hint] Difference between Heat and Temperature
> - **Temperature**: the _average_ kinetic energy per particle. It tells you how fast the "typical" particle is moving.
> - **Heat**: the _total_ amount of kinetic energy that actually moves from the hot object to the cold one.


## Heat formula

$$\Large Q=mC\Delta T$$

Where:
- $Q$ is the heat required to raise mass $m$ by $\Delta T$ degrees.
- $c$ is the specific heat, which is the heat required to raise the temperature of one unit of mass by $1°C$.

---

## Latent Heat

Heat required to make mass change phase, after the phase change temperature has been reached.
All heat goes the the phase change, temperature doesn't raise again until it's finished.

$$\Large Q=Lm$$
where $L$ is the specific latent heat (heat required for one unit of mass of substance to change phase).
