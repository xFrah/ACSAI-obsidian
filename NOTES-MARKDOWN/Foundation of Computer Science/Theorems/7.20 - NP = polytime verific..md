
> [!note] [[16. P vs NP#NP|NP]] definition
> The set of languages that are decided from a nondeterministic turing machineÂ in polynomial time.
> A machine can be nondeterministic and decide in polynomial time, since the length of the correct guess is polynomial with respect to the length of the input. All guesses are tried at the same time.

> [!note] [[16. P vs NP#Verifier|Verifier]] definition
> A verifier is a deterministic turing machine that can tell whether a "guess" (c) is a solution or not in polynomial time (for problem instance $w$).


A verifier is deterministic, but the NP definition tells us about a non deterministic decider. We will show that the two definitions are the same.

> [!hint] Hint by the professor
> We have two definitions of NP problems here:
> - On one side, we have the **whole tree of non-deterministic guesses**.
> - On the other side, we have the **verifier**, and **we take into consideration only one branch**.
> 
> We show that the solutions given by those two models are the same.

## $\rightarrow$ (construct the verifier from the NTM)

We need to construct the verifier from the existing Non-Deterministic TM that accepts it.
We just take the Turing machine, we simulate it on problem $w$ and we make it follow $c$ step by step.
If the TM accepts, the verifier accepts.

## $\leftarrow$ (construct the NTM from the verifier)

We need to construct the Non-Deterministic TM from an existing verifier.
Run infinitely many instances of the verifier over all the possible candidates, if one accepts, accept.
